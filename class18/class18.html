Q3. Describe what happened after the introduction of the aP vaccine? Do you have a possible explanation for the observed trend?

After the introduction of the aP vaccine in 1996, pertussis cases began increasing again over time. One possible explanation is that immunity from the aP vaccine wanes faster than immunity from the older wP vaccine. Other possible explanations include increased vaccine hesitancy, improved PCR-based detection methods, and bacterial evolution leading to escape from vaccine-induced immunity.

Q14. What antigens show differences in the level of IgG antibody titers recognizing them over time? Why these and not others?

Antigens such as PT, FHA, and PRN show clear increases in IgG antibody titers over time after boosting. These antigens are components of pertussis vaccines, so the immune system recognizes and responds strongly to them. Control antigens that are not part of the vaccine show much smaller changes over time.

Q21. Does this pattern in time match the trend of antibody titer data? If not, why not?

The RNA expression pattern does not perfectly match antibody titer levels because RNA expression occurs earlier than production and accumulation of antibodies. Antibodies are proteins that can remain in circulation for longer periods after gene expression decreases.
